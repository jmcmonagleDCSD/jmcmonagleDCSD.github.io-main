What do Platypuses eat?

Platypuses eat a variety of insect larvae, yabbies, small fish and worms. They find their food amongst the rocks, leaf litter and other material on the bottom of the water body with their bill.

Platypuses also eat freshwater shrimp and may catch adult insects on the surface of the water.

Feeding and Diet

The Platypus mainly eats during the night on a wide variety of aquatic invertebrates.

The average foraging periods last for 10-12 hours per day, and the distances the animals move during this time vary between individuals and their distribution.

The bill of the Platypus serves to find and sift small prey from the substrate, while larger prey is taken individually.

What Hunts Platypuses?

Platypuses are eaten by snakes, water rats, birds of prey and occasionally crocodiles. It's likely that foxes, dingoes and wild dogs kill Platypuses that explore on land.

What also hunts platypuses? Predators like owls, eagles, and hawks may prey upon them. Threats like dingoes, Tasmanian devils, monitor lizards, snakes, and water rats also await.
